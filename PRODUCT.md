# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

UK recruiters, hiring managers, and engineering leads reviewing Ideal Rafuna for systems engineering, simulation, digital twin, robotics, UAV, automotive systems, integration, validation, and software-for-physical-systems roles. They are scanning quickly for role fit, work authorization, credible engineering depth, and project proof.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio exists to position Ideal Rafuna as an employer-ready systems-focused aerospace and mechanical engineer who can connect physical systems, simulation, software, data, and real-time architecture. Success means a reviewer understands the role fit within seconds, trusts the engineering evidence, sees UK work authorization clearly, and has a low-friction path to the resume and contact channels.

## Brand Personality

Precise, technical, and composed. The site should feel like a premium engineering portfolio rather than an academic biography or startup pitch. It should convey practical depth, systems thinking, and credible delivery without becoming loud, generic, or decorative.

## Anti-references

Do not make the portfolio feel like a university application, professor-facing research profile, founder-first startup pitch, or generic SaaS landing page. Avoid degree-application copy, university-application language, repeated academic section labels, excessive gradient decoration, generic glass cards, childish visuals, and unverified performance claims.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with employer signal: role fit, UK work authorization, and strongest project proof must be visible before a recruiter starts hunting.
2. Show systems thinking structurally: navigation, project architecture, metrics, and case-study details should reveal how physical systems, software, simulation, and data connect.
3. Preserve research as evidence: publications and research projects stay, but they support engineering depth rather than becoming the main narrative.
4. Make TelloTwin the proof object: the UAV digital twin should anchor the portfolio with architecture, telemetry, interfaces, and validation-relevant metrics.
5. Keep polish disciplined: use crisp typography, restrained motion, strong spacing, accessible contrast, and technical artifacts instead of decorative noise.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-visible focus, responsive layouts down to mobile, readable text without horizontal scrolling, descriptive image alt text, and reduced-motion support for non-essential movement. Dark and light modes must remain usable and coherent.
